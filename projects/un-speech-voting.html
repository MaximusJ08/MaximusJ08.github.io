UN speech sentiment and Palestine voting

A predictive modelling project testing whether UN General Debate speeches, Palestine-related mentions, sentiment, region, and recognition status can explain or predict votes on Palestine-related resolutions.

GitHub Open report Download report Back to projects

What I Built

The project joined UN General Debate text with voting records and engineered Palestine-specific speech features. The workflow used keyword filtering, TF-IDF term selection, VADER sentiment augmentation, country-level contextual variables, and supervised classifiers including Random Forest, Naive Bayes, and Logistic Regression.

The strongest signal was not sentiment alone. The report concludes that geography, recognition status, and how often Palestine was mentioned were more informative than sentiment polarity, while class imbalance made minority-class F1 a more useful target than headline accuracy.

Project Signals

  • UN General Debate corpus and General Assembly voting records.
  • Palestine mention detection, TF-IDF vocabulary analysis, and sentiment scoring.
  • Random Forest model tuned for imbalanced vote outcomes.
  • Interpretation focused on structural alliances as well as political rhetoric.
